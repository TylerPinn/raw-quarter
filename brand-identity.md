## 01 — The Concept

Raw Quarter is a premium essentials brand built around a single product done exceptionally well: the quarter zip.

In a market saturated with everything-brands, Raw Quarter does one thing. The quarter zip is having a cultural moment — driven by Gen Z on social media, adopted by everyone from the young creative to the business professional. We're here at the start of that wave, owning the niche before it gets crowded.

The name does the work quietly. *Raw* speaks to honesty — no excess, no performance, just quality. *Quarter* is both the product and the logo — a circle with a quarter missing, an icon that reads as R, as Q, as a garment detail. It doesn't explain itself. It doesn't need to.

## 03 — Brand Story

The quarter zip is not new. But owning it — designing every detail of one garment, launching a brand *named after* it — that's a different move.

Raw Quarter starts from a simple belief: that one well-made thing, worn consistently, says more than ten average things worn occasionally. The quarter zip is versatile by nature. You can layer it over a shirt for dinner. You can wear it Sunday morning at home. It works. It's always worked.

What's changed is the audience. Gen Z has rediscovered the quarter zip — not as the suburban dad staple it once was, but as an intentional choice. A silhouette that's clean, slightly structured, effortlessly elevated. Raw Quarter is here to give that instinct a brand worth belonging to.

## 04 — The Customer

**Primary:** Men & women, 18–34  
**Mindset:** Values quality over quantity. Prefers a minimal wardrobe of pieces that work. Wears brands that don't shout. Influenced by social media but not defined by it. Appreciates craft without needing to explain why.

**Two personas:**

**The Elevating Minimalist** *(22–28)*  
Lives in a capital city. Works in creative, tech, or hospitality. Curates their wardrobe the same way they curate their Spotify. Wants pieces that photograph well but don't look like they tried. Finds something like this, tells two friends.

**The Everyday Premium Guy** *(28–38)*  
Has a job he cares about. Doesn't want to think too hard about what to wear but wants to look good. Values the ease of a great essential. Has moved past logo-heavy pieces. This is exactly his brand — he just hasn't found it yet.

## 05 — Visual Identity

### Logo System

Raw Quarter operates a four-part logo system:

**Primary Logo** — Icon + stacked wordmark lockup. Main identifier. Dark or light background.  
**Secondary Logo** — Icon centred above stacked wordmark. Compact applications, swing tags, labels.  
**Icon Logo** — Standalone Q/R mark. Social avatars, embroidery, embossed details.  
**Wordmark** — RAWQUARTER set as a single entity. Wide-tracked, full-width applications.

### The Icon

The icon is a geometric Q formed from approximately 270° of a circle — three-quarters complete, one quarter open. Inside the arc, a secondary curve creates negative space that reads simultaneously as:

- The letter **R** (brand initial)
- The letter **Q** (product reference)
- A **circle with a quarter removed** (literal product detail — the quarter zip opening)

It is a mark that rewards attention without demanding it.

### Colour Palette

| Swatch | Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| ⬛ | Raw Black | #1A1A1A | Primary brand colour. Logos, type, main colourway. |
| ⬜ | Clean White | #F5F5F5 | Reversed logos, light background applications. |
| 🩶 | Grey Marle | #9B9B9B | Secondary UI, supporting tone. |
| 🟤 | Quarter Tan | #C4A882 | Earth tone accent. Seasonal product colourways. |
| 🫒 | Dusty Olive | #7D8471 | Earth tone accent. Seasonal product colourways. |
| 🟫 | Raw Brown | #6B4F3A | Earth tone accent. Campaign and editorial use. |

**Core product colourways (launch):** Black / Grey Marle / Navy / Tan / Olive

### Typography

**Primary Typeface:** Geometric sans-serif — bold weight, all caps, extended letter-spacing (tracking: +100–150)  
**Application:** All brand touchpoints. Headlines, labels, hang tags, packaging.  
**Tone:** The tracking is intentional. It slows the reader down. It signals confidence. It is not in a hurry.

**Supporting Type:** Clean, lightweight sans-serif for body copy (size labels, care instructions, website copy). Never competes with the wordmark.

### Visual Direction

- **Photography:** Editorial-clean studio shots + lifestyle imagery in natural light. Neutral backgrounds. No noise, no clutter.
- **Styling:** The garment is the focus. Minimal accessories. Pieces that could exist in anyone's wardrobe.
- **Mood:** Understated confidence. The person wearing it isn't trying to be seen — they just are.

## 06 — Voice & Tone

**Three words:** Minimal. Honest. Considered.

Raw Quarter doesn't oversell. The copy is short. The statements are direct. We believe the product speaks — the words just introduce it.

**Examples:**

> *One garment. Worn every way.*

> *The quarter zip, done right.*

> *Raw Quarter. That's it.*

**What we don't say:**  
- "Luxury" (aspirational inflation)  
- "For everyone" (positioning dilution)  
- "Premium quality" without showing it

## 07 — Product Direction

**Hero Product:** The Quarter Zip  
**Launch Colourways:** Black, Grey Marle, Navy, Tan, Olive  
**Fabrication Target:** Heavyweight fleece / French terry. Quality hand-feel is non-negotiable.  
**Construction Notes:** Clean seams, minimal external branding, quality zipper hardware. The icon logo embroidered at chest — subtle placement.  
**Sizing:** Unisex sizing system. Inclusive range.

**Future product extensions (Year 2+):**  
Tee, hoodie, track pant — only if quality standard is maintained. Raw Quarter expands slowly and deliberately.

## 08 — Ghost Builder Brand Incubator — Fit Assessment

Raw Quarter is an ideal first test case for the Brand Incubator model for the following reasons:

**1. Single-SKU simplicity**  
One product in multiple colourways reduces complexity across the entire supply chain — from sampling through to fulfilment. It is the cleanest possible test of the end-to-end model.

**2. Future Fulfilment alignment**  
Cut-and-sew production (fleece quarter zips) is a natural fit for the Future Fulfilment centre in China. Holding pre-production stock at a third-party warehouse and shipping DTC aligns precisely with the model being tested.

**3. DTG adjacency**  
While the hero product is cut-and-sew, Raw Quarter's minimal-branding approach means future graphic tees or accessory pieces would be a natural DTG play — keeping the brand within Ghost Builder's full production capability.

**4. Social-proof growth path**  
The quarter zip trend is live on TikTok and Instagram right now. A focused launch — right product, right time, right look — can generate organic reach without paid media. This validates the Brand Incubator model's ability to launch culturally relevant brands.

**5. Scalability template**  
If Raw Quarter works, every subsequent Brand Incubator project has a proven playbook: focused product category, strong visual identity, DTC-first, Future Fulfilment-enabled.
